Manifesto · §01

The dealership runs on fifty tabs and a prayer.
We think it should run on a sentence.

01 → 06 · The premise

A store does two hundred things a day. Chase a lead. Approve a deal. Reprice a unit. Send a follow-up. Post a GL entry. Tell payroll that Marcus closed three.

Today those two hundred things live in nine different logins and the tribal knowledge of whoever's been there longest. Driveline collapses all of them into one surface: a chat.

Ask for what you want. It reaches into your systems, drafts the change, shows its work, and waits for your yes. It learns the way your store actually closes deals — and runs the parts you used to forget.

§04 · Automation builder

Build workflows
by describing them.

Forget node-by-node flow charts. Tell Driveline what should happen and when. It assembles the trigger, the conditions, and the outcome — and shows you the graph before it turns on.

/01
Triggers, in your words

"Any trade appraised over $18k." "A lead that hasn't been touched in 36 hours." No event picker. No cron syntax.

/02
Conditions that branch

Layer rules the way you actually think: source, stock age, salesperson, gross. Driveline compiles the boolean for you.

/03
Actions across your stack

Post a task in your CRM. Text the UCM. Update the deal. Re-run payroll splits. One flow, many systems.

/04
Preview, then ship

Every automation dry-runs against the last 30 days of real data so you see exactly what will fire — before it does.

§09 · FAQ

Answers, before
you ask them.

Does Driveline replace my DMS or CRM?
No. Driveline sits on top of what you already run. It reads and writes to your DMS, CRM, accounting, and payroll through their APIs — but your systems of record stay put. Think of Driveline as the single chair everyone sits in to reach all of them.
What if it makes a mistake?
Every action requires a human green-light by default. Drafts wait for approval. Automations dry-run against the last 30 days before they go live. And every change is logged and reversible — you see the diff before anything ships.
How long does it take to get live?
Most single-rooftop stores are running in under two weeks. Integration authentication is guided. We mirror a week of your historical data, tune the voice to your store, then ship with a success lead watching the first month.
Who at the store actually uses it?
Sales reps draft follow-ups and ask for lead context. UCMs price and approve from the chat. F&I runs menus. The GM uses it for the view — "how are we doing today?" — and to rewrite the sales flow when something's not working.
Is our customer data safe?
Driveline is SOC 2 Type II and signs a BAA. Your data is segregated per rooftop, encrypted at rest, and — on Fleet — never leaves a private inference cloud. We never use store data to train shared models.
What does "engineer the sales flow" really mean?
It means your process — the steps a deal moves through, the nudges it fires, the people it touches — is software you can rewrite in natural language. "Move leads older than 48 hours to the BDC." Done. No tickets, no admin console archaeology.
